To help us figure out what comes next we are going to make a new page. We call it the Dev Page.

How to create the Dev Page

  • Create a TABLE like the one below. The top row and the left column should look just like the table below.
  • Your table needs to have an alternating color scheme (like the one below), but you do not have to use the same colors we did. {we call our scheme   RASPBERRY PIE   }
  • Inside your table, you need to write (type) in answers to all the questions that you can. For the questions you cannot answer, you can leave them blank. The statement in the Row Header is what the questions address. The questions that make up the table below are ones that we came up with to help us explore the Header statements. If you do not like our questions, you can create your own. The only requirement is that you have four questions in your row. Those questions must address the Header statement. You can have text consisting of questions and answers in your table. Or you can add the questions as comments in your code and only have the text showing be the answers to the questions.
  • The part of the header for your table that says {aka a communication plan} must be a link back to THIS page. (the one we are currently on)
  • You also need to add LINKS on both this page and your homepage that connect them. The link on your homepage can be a "Secret Link." Secret Links: links that do not have link colors or text decorations. The link could be just a period or text that is the same color as your background. Thus showing up as a blank spot that only you know is there. The link will take you to your Dev Page.
  • Be sure to EMAIL us the files you used to make your table along with any updates to your homepage so we can update your page.
  • We put a secret link to our Dev Page on our HOMEPAGE. Bet you cant find it ;)


Dream Project Development Plan {aka a communication plan}
GOALS: What are your goals? (make sure your goals are specific, measureable, attainable, relevant and timely) What are your coding goals? Why are these your goals? What small pieces make up your goals? How does the completion of your goals look? What can you create that would express the completion of some of your coding goals? (choose one thing to start off with as your first Dream Project you can have as many as you like but we need to start somewhere)
PROJECTS: What message do you need to communicate? Why? What does your Dream Project look like fully completed? What does your Dream Project look like broken down into smaller parts? What do you need to know to create the smallest part of your Dream Project? What message are you trying to communicate with this project?
FEED-BACK: What actions/attitudes do you hope others will adopt Who do you most want to see your finished project? Why? What reaction do you want your audience to have when they experience your completed project? How can you try and ensure that your audience has the reaction that you want? How will you be able to tell what reactions your audience is having to your project?
MEDIUM: which communication channels do you intend to use? (in person online marketing materials etc)/ What communication mediums do you intend to utilize? (email if you chose online) How does your Dream Project look? What makes it look that way? How will you make the different aspects of your project look the way they do? Digital manipulation, Physical creation, Both, Neither, Something else? Are you using multiple vehicles to reach your audience? For example, do you have a blog, a newsletter, and a website, or do you just have a website? Why or why not? Could you change how your audience engages with your project? What difference would that make?
MILESTONES: what are the milestones of your message and when should you reach them? What tasks do you need to complete? Which ones are more important? Why? What accomplishments would you consider most important in your project timeline? Do you have a specific timeline in which you need (or want) to be done with certain aspects of your project? Could setting up a reward system for reaching certain points in your project be benaficial? What would that look like?
ENGAGEMENT: how frequently do you expect to be in contact with your audience? How often does your audience interact with your project? Is your audience's interaction dependent on you putting out some sort of content? Is audience interaction dependent only on some sort of advertisement plan? Why or why not? What encourages people to interact with your project? Do you have a call to action for your users? Why or why not?
EVALUATION: how will you evaluate your efforts? What does the success of this project look like to you? Why? How can you express the success of your project with measurements? How will you know that this project is complete, or does this project have an endpoint? What does the failure of this project look like to you? Why?
