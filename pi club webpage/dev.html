Time to Plan!

Now you have a web page. Good Work! Now its time to add some more pages. What do you want to put out there online? Some people come just knowing that they want to do, a lot more than some dont though. Below is a tool you can use to help you narrow down the specifics of what you want to do.

  • Create a Nesting of Lists like the one below.
  • Your Lists needs to have an alternating color scheme (like the one below), but you do not have to use the same colors we did. {we call our scheme   Raspberry - Pie   }
  • Inside your Lists, you need to type in answers to all the questions that you can. For the questions you cannot answer, you can leave them blank.
  • You need to add links on both this page and your homepage that connect the two pages to each other. The link on your homepage can be a "Secret" Link*.
  • Be sure to bring your files to the next Club Meeting that you used to make your table along with any updates to your homepage so you can update your page.
  • We put a "Secret" Link that connects to our Dev Page on the homepage. Bet you cant find it ;)


*Secret Links: links that do not have link colors or text decorations. The link could be just a period or text that is the same color as your background. Thus showing up as a blank spot that only you know is there. The link will take you to your Dev Page.

    • Specific: What do you want to create with code? Why?

    • Measureable: What components do you need to develop what you would like to make with code. ex. To make a pizza you need the following components. Dough, Sause, Cheese, Toppings, and Oven Heat.

    • Attainable: Based off what you already know and what you have learned that you need to know to accomplish your goal. What can you fully complete in the next month that will give you a sense of accomplishent towards your goal?
      • . . . . .
    • Relevant: How does this goal relate to you? ex. To answer this you can ponder on the following. How does this goal fit into your personal life? How does it fit into your professional or educational life? How does it fit into the lives of the people you care about?
      • . . . . .
    • Timely: At this point how much time do you think it would take to finish something in relation to this project that would leave you with a sense of accomplishment? How can you keep yourself accountable to that timeline?

  • Project:
    • Specific:Write up an outline for your project. Have it include:
      • A summary of what your project is
      • the steps you need to complete to finish your project
      • your desired outcome
      • . . . . .
    • Measureable: Create a scale that you can use to determine how well your project is progressing.ex. If your goal is to run a marathon you can rate your progress by how much distance you can add to your runs every week.
      • . . . . .
    • Attainable: Do you have all the skills you need to complete this project? If not, how long would it take you to gain those skills? Is that timeline attainable? Can you make that timeline more attainable?
      • . . . . .
    • Relevant: How does this project help you achive your overal coding goals?
      • . . . . .
    • Timely: Create a timeline for this project. Is there anyway you can break this project down into smaller projects so you can accomplish more in less time?
      • . . . . .
  • Feedback:
    • Specific: Who will be interacting with your project, is it just for you or will you share it with others. How would you like to get feedback about your project?
      • . . . . .
    • Measureable: How can you measure the feedback that you get from your project in a way that is able to be expressed or measured as a quantity?
      • . . . . .
    • Attainable: What does your ideal feedback data look like? Can you break that down into a smaller quantity?
      • . . . . .
    • Relevant: How will this feedback data be used in regards to your project?ex. Getting feedback from a taste test may help the cook change the flavor profile of their dish.
      • . . . . .
    • Timely: How long will you recive feedback for this project? What factors, if any will change how the feedback is used or interpreted and will that affect your timeline for feedback?
      • . . . . .
  • Milestones:
    • Specific: Do you need a timeline for your main coding goals, your project coding goals or both? If you have multiple projects that make up your main goal how do those timelines interact?
      • . . . . .
    • Measureable: Is there a way that you can track how well you are keeping to your timeline? Can you add a level of accountability to that tracking method?
      • . . . . .
    • Attainable: After you create a timeline make one that is too quick and one that is too slow. How does your original stand up to the fast and slow timelines?
      • . . . . .
    • Relevant: What are some things that have just happen unexpect in the past to you. Did you plan for those types of accidentals in your timeline, since "life happnes"?
      • . . . . .
    • Timely: Set up an accountability system to go along with your timeline. What will "keep you honest" when it comes to hitting milestones on time or shortly after?
      • . . . . .
  • Engagement:
    • Specific: Will people besides yourself interact with your project? If so, how so?
      • . . . . .
    • Measureable: Is there a way to track what specific parts of your project people interact with the most? Or how long they interact with your project at a time?
      • . . . . .
    • Attainable:What is your goal level of engagment with your project? Why? Make a list of how can you realistically make those level happen?
      • . . . . .
    • Relevant: How relevant to your project that people engage with it? What type of engagement is relevant to your project?
      • . . . . .
    • Timely: Is there a cut off point for engagement for your project, does it end at some point or does it go on forever? Would it be benificial to add that timeline to your overall milestones timeline?
      • . . . . .
  • Evaluation:
    • Specific:What specific aspects of your project make it successful to you? What aspects make it unsuccessful?
      • . . . . .
    • Measureable: Is there any way you can express or measure these aspects as quantities to help determine the overall effectiveness of your project?
      • . . . . .
    • Attainable: What min and max levels of these aspects will make your project successfull? What min and max will make your project unsucessful? Are the ranges that you came up with realistic in regards to the resources that you have?
      • . . . . .
    • Relevant: How will this feedback directly help you to modify your project? Are the measure you are using to track your success following along the lines of information that is relevant to your improvement of the project or overall coding goals?
      • . . . . .
    • Timely: At what point will information that is helpful to the improvement of your project not be relevant anymore? At that point can this information be used in a different way or is it no longer required to collect? Can you add that timeline to your milestones timeline?
